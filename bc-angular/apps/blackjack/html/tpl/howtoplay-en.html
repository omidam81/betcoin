Playing Casino Blackjack

  1. Blackjack is played with eight decks of 52-card decks.

  2. Aces may be counted as 1 or 11 points, 2 to 9 according to pip value, and tens and face cards count as ten points.

  3. The value of a hand is the sum of the point values of the individual cards. Except, a "blackjack" is the highest hand, consisting of an ace and any 10-point card, and it outranks all other 21-point hands.

  4. If the dealer has a blackjack, then all wagers will lose, unless the player also has a blackjack, which will result in a push.

  5. If the player got 21 un-natural hand, it will push when the dealer got 21 un-natural hand as well, or it will lose the wager when dealer got a Blackjack.

  6. After the player has finished his turn, the dealer will turn over his hole card. If the dealer has 16 or less, then he will draw another card. A special situation is when the dealer has an ace and any number of cards totaling six points (known as a "soft 17").

  7. If the dealer goes over 21 points, then any player who didn't already bust will win.

  8. If the dealer does not bust, then the higher point total between the player and dealer will win.

  9. Winning wagers pay even money, except a winning player blackjack pays 3 to 2.

Game Options:

  1. Stand: Player stands pat with his cards.

  2. Hit: Player draws another card (and more if he wishes). If this card causes the player's total points to exceed 21 (known as "breaking" or "busting") then he loses.

  3. Double: Player with 10 or 11 total score, is eligible to double his bet and gets one, and only one, more card.

  4. Split: If the player has a pair, or any two 10-point cards, then he may double his bet and separate his cards into two individual hands. The dealer will automatically give each card a second card. Then, the player may hit, stand. However, player can't split on Aces, and can't hit anymore when there is a split ace. Player can only split to two hands.
